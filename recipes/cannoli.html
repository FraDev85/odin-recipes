How to prepare Cannoli

Let's start

Ingredients

For the shells (makes ~10-12 medium cannoli):

  • 250 g all-purpose flour
  • 30g sugar
  • 30 g lard or butter
  • 1 egg yolk
  • 60 ml dry white wine or Marsala
  • 1 pinch of salt
  • olive oil for fry

For filling

  • 500 g ricotta (preferably sheep’s milk, very well drained)
  • 150–200 g powdered sugar (to taste)
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract or orange zest (optional)
  • 50–70 g dark chocolate chips or candied orange peel

Decoration (optional):

  • Chopped pistachios
  • Candied cherries
  • Powdered sugar for dusting

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the shells dough
    1. In a bowl, mix flour, sugar, salt
    2. Add lard (or butter) and mix until crumbly.
    3. Add the egg yolk and wine gradually; knead until smooth (about 10 min).
    4. Wrap in plastic and let it rest in the fridge for 1–2 hours.
  2. Make the filling
    1. Drain the ricotta overnight in the fridge, if possible.
    2. Pass it through a fine sieve for a creamy texture.
    3. Mix with powdered sugar, vanilla/orange zest, and chocolate chips.
    4. Chill until ready to use.
  3. Roll out and fry the shells
    1. Roll dough thinly (about 1–2 mm) and cut into ovals or 10×10 cm squares.
    2. Wrap each around a metal cannoli tube, sealing the edge with egg white.
    3. Heat oil to ~170–180°C (340–360°F), fry until golden (1–2 min).
    4. Drain on paper towels. Cool before removing the tubes.
  4. Assemble
    1. Just before serving, fill shells with ricotta cream using a piping bag.
    2. Decorate ends with pistachios, candied fruit, or chocolate.
    3. Dust with powdered sugar.
