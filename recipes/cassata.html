How to prepare Cassata

Lets start:

Ingredients:

  • For the sponge cake (Pan di Spagna):
  • 6 eggs
  • 180 g sugar
  • 180 g all-purpose flour
  • Zest of 1 lemon or vanilla
  • Pinch of salt
  • For the ricotta cream:
  • 700 g sheep’s milk ricotta (very well drained)
  • 250 g powdered sugar
  • 100 g dark chocolate chips or chopped chocolate
  • Optional: candied orange peel, vanilla
  • For the marzipan (pasta reale):
  • 200 g almond flour
  • 200 g powdered sugar
  • ~40 ml water
  • Green food coloring (traditional)
  • For decoration:
  • Candied fruit (cherries, citrus peel, etc.)
  • Optional: icing sugar or a sugar glaze
  • For Assemble:
  • Liqueur or sugar syrup (to moisten the sponge)
  • Apricot jam (optional, for glazing)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the sponge cake
    1. Beat eggs and sugar until very fluffy (at least 10 minutes).
    2. Gently fold in sifted flour and lemon zest.
    3. Bake at 180°C (350°F) for ~30 min in a greased, floured round pan.
    4. Let cool completely and slice into thin layers.
  2. Prepare the ricotta filling
    1. Sieve the ricotta or beat it until smooth.
    2. Mix with powdered sugar, then stir in chocolate chips and candied peel.
    3. Chill in the fridge.
  3. Prepare the marzipan
    1. Mix almond flour and powdered sugar.
    2. Add water gradually to form a dough.
    3. Knead with green coloring until smooth.
    4. Roll out into ~0.5 cm sheets.
  4. Assemble the cassata
    1. Line a round mold (or springform pan) with plastic wrap.
    2. Line the sides with green marzipan strips.
    3. Place sponge cake slices on the bottom and sides.
    4. Moisten sponge with syrup or liqueur (e.g., Maraschino or orange liqueur).
    5. Fill with ricotta cream.
    6. Top with more sponge and gently press down.
    7. Chill for several hours (or overnight).
  5. Finish and decorate
    1. Turn cassata out of the mold onto a tray.
    2. Optionally cover with a glaze made of powdered sugar + water.
    3. Decorate with candied fruit in geometric patterns.
